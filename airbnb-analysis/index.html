How Property Characteristics Influence Airbnb Prices and Occupancy

Specific Question: How do property characteristics (number of bedrooms, bathrooms, beds, accommodates, property type, and room type) influence Airbnb listing prices and occupancy rates across major U.S. cities?

##

Introduction

This report examines the characteristics of Airbnb listings across major U.S. cities. In my analytical process, I chose to focus on three major cities: New York City, Chicago, and Los Angeles. The analysis aims to explore relationships between property features and two outcomes: listing prices and occupancy rates. Additionally, insights are derived to understand the distribution of average prices and occupancy rates across the most common property types in each city. The report is organized into four main sections: data acquisition and processing, regression modeling, aggregation and visualization of key metrics, and interpretation of results.

1. Data Acquisition and Initial Cleaning

The datasets analyzed consist of Airbnb listing information provided as CSV files from the InsideAirbnb data source. Each dataset contains the variables of listing price, availability, property characteristics (number of bedrooms, bathrooms, and beds), and property type. To make the report replicable, I created a custom function “load_city_data” that accepts the file path and a city name, and returns a cleaned data frame with relevant variables transformed.

library(tidyverse)
library(plotly)

# Function to load and clean a city's data
load_city_data <- function(file_path, city_name) {
  read_csv(file_path, show_col_types = FALSE) %>%
    mutate(
      # Removes '$' and ','
      price = as.numeric(str_remove_all(as.character(price), "[$,]")),
      
      # Calculate occupancy rate using availability_365
      occupancy_rate = 1 - (availability_365 / 365),
      
      bathrooms = as.numeric(bathrooms),
      city = city_name
    ) %>%
    # Remove outliers
    filter(!is.na(price), price > 0, price < 1000)
}

The process of cleaning the data included:

  1. Price conversion: Prices are initially stored as character strings with ‘$’ and ‘,’ symbols. These are removed, and the column is converted into numerics.

  2. Occupancy rate calculation: Given the column ‘availability_365’, the variable occupancy_rate is derived by: occupancy_rate = 1 - (availability_365 / 365). This gives us the proportion of days a property is booked. 

  3. Bathroom conversion: Bathrooms are converted to numerics, as some entries may contain text descriptors.

  4. Filtering: Properties with missing or unrealistic prices (less than 0 or over $1000) are removed. This threshold ensures extreme outliers do not bias the analysis. 

This function is then applied to each city:

3.1 Interpretation of Results

In the price chart, “Entire home” and “Entire rental unit” listings consistently show the highest average prices, confirming the strong positive coefficients seen in the regression. In the occupancy chart, “Private room” listings exhibit competitive occupancy rates across all cities, matching the positive coefficients from the occupancy model for the variable “Private room”. Chicago shows the highest average occupancy overall, suggesting a more balanced supply-demand dynamic, while New York shows the highest prices but relatively lower occupancy. These findings illustrate the trade-off between price and utilization predicted by the regression models: higher-priced property types tend to exhibit lower occupancy rates, while lower-priced, smaller listings achieve steadier booking frequency.

4. Conclusion of Results

This study shows that property characteristics are major determinants of price but minor determinants of occupancy. Bathrooms and bedrooms strongly increase price, while smaller, affordable listings secure higher utilization. The two regression models and their accompanying visualizations reveal a clear economic balance, hosts of larger or luxury properties can expect higher prices but lower booking frequency, and smaller or shared units maximize occupancy at lower rates. By combining regression analysis with visualization, this report highlights the dual impact of structural features on both pricing strategy and booking performance across major U.S. Airbnb markets.

4.1 Limitations

Despite the strength of these relationships, several limitations must still be acknowledged:

  1. The analysis approximated occupancy using “1 - availability_365 / 365”, which treats unavailable days as booked. This method cannot distinguish between actual reservations and host-blocked availability, potentially overstating occupancy in some cases.

  2. Neighborhood effects, proximity to locations such as trains, schools or parks, and seasonal demand variation were not included in the models. These factors likely explain the portion of the variation in occupancy and price not mentioned in this report.

  3. The data represents a single time frame and cannot capture trends, or major changes in markets like the pandemic.

These limitations highlight that while property characteristics are essential predictors of price, they capture only part of the complex economic behavior underlying Airbnb demand.

4.2 Future Work

To strengthen and expand this analysis, several methodological improvements could be done:

  1. Future models should integrate geographical data, and seasonal variables to control for location-specific and seasonal variations in demand.

  2. Rather than relying on the availability counts, further analysis could have been with a calendar csv data to calculate booking rates per listing, providing a more accurate occupancy rate.

  3. Expanding visualization to be more dynamic and interactive.

4.3 Final Reflection

This study provides clear empirical evidence that physical property characteristics are strong determinants of price but weaker predictors of occupancy. These findings suggest that hosts and investors face a trade-off between maximizing price per night and achieving consistent booking frequency. Airbnb owners therefore have to decide depending on individual priorities. Owners of larger and more luxurious properties, can set a premium price to offset the lower occupancy, while smaller and more private room hosts can achieve a more steady occupancy and revenue through affordability. Ultimately while property traits set the foundation for value, market context, location, and seasonality remain decisive factors in Airbnb performance. By refining future models to incorporate location and seasonal dimensions, researchers can gain a better understanding of the economic drivers shaping the Airbnb market across major U.S cities.
